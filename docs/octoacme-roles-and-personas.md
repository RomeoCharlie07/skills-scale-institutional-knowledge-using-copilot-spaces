# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, coach teams on process improvements, and help remove impediments that block progress. They ensure the team follows agile best practices while fostering a culture of continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (sprint planning, daily standups, retrospectives, demos)
- Coach team members on agile practices and mindset
- Identify and help remove impediments blocking the team
- Shield the team from external distractions and scope creep
- Track and communicate team velocity and capacity
- Promote continuous improvement through retrospectives

### Goals
- Enable consistent, predictable delivery cadences
- Foster a culture of transparency and continuous improvement
- Maintain team focus and reduce context-switching
- Ensure agile principles are understood and followed

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Align on delivery timelines, escalate cross-team dependencies, and coordinate resource needs
- **With Product Managers**: Ensure backlog refinement happens regularly and acceptance criteria are clear before sprint planning
- **With Developers**: Facilitate daily standups, address blockers, and coach on iteration best practices
- **With QA Lead**: Coordinate testing activities within sprints and ensure quality gates are met

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams. They elicit, analyze, and document requirements to ensure solutions meet business needs and deliver expected value.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into user stories and acceptance criteria
- Create process flows, wireframes, and requirement specifications
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements
- Facilitate requirement workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Maintain traceability between requirements and delivered features

### Goals
- Ensure clear, unambiguous requirements that reduce rework
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Validate solutions deliver expected business value
- Reduce scope ambiguity and misalignment

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Align on scope definition, timeline impacts of requirement changes, and stakeholder communication
- **With Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature prioritization, clarify scope and acceptance criteria
- **With Developers**: Clarify requirements, answer questions during implementation, and validate acceptance criteria
- **With Stakeholders**: Conduct interviews, gather feedback, and validate business requirements

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered experiences by researching user needs, designing intuitive interfaces, and validating solutions through testing. They ensure products are usable, accessible, and deliver customer value.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define and maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design
- Validate designs through user feedback and metrics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve task completion rates
- Maintain design consistency across the product
- Ensure designs are validated before development

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Align on user needs, feature requirements, and success metrics for user experience
- **With Developers**: Collaborate on implementation feasibility, provide design specifications and assets
- **With Business Analysts**: Understand user workflows and business requirements to inform design decisions
- **With QA Lead**: Ensure designs are testable and acceptance criteria cover usability aspects

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain documentation for internal teams and external users. They ensure processes, decisions, and product features are captured clearly throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create user-facing documentation (guides, tutorials, API references)
- Maintain internal process and onboarding documentation
- Document release artifacts, changelogs, and migration guides
- Collaborate with teams to capture architectural decisions and technical specs
- Review and improve documentation quality and consistency
- Ensure documentation stays current with product changes

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear, self-service documentation
- Accelerate onboarding for new team members
- Capture institutional knowledge and reduce key-person dependencies
- Maintain documentation accuracy and currency

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinate documentation timelines with releases and ensure process docs are current
- **With Product Managers**: Understand feature changes and user needs for documentation
- **With Developers**: Gather technical details, review accuracy, and document APIs and integrations
- **With QA Lead**: Ensure test documentation and quality standards are captured

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads oversee the testing strategy and ensure quality standards are met across the project. They coordinate testing activities, define acceptance criteria validation, and partner with developers throughout implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy (unit, integration, E2E, security)
- Establish and enforce quality gates and acceptance criteria validation
- Coordinate manual and automated testing activities
- Review and approve test coverage for releases
- Identify quality risks and recommend mitigations
- Mentor QA team members and promote testing best practices

### Goals
- Ensure consistent quality across all deliverables
- Minimize production defects and regressions
- Maintain high test coverage and test automation
- Enable faster, confident releases through quality gates

### Typical Communication/Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Report on quality status, test coverage, and release readiness
- **With Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria are testable and align on quality priorities
- **With Developers**: Partner on test automation, code reviews for testability, and bug triage
- **With Scrum Master**: Coordinate testing activities within sprint cycles
